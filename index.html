About Bike Sharing

People living in densely populated urban areas are increasingly concerned about climate change, the over-dependence on fossil fuel in our daily lives, and air polution. More and more urban dwellers are interested in developing sustainable transporation alternatives to mitigate these uncertainties facing our planet. The International Transport Forum predicts that by 2050, the world will have to contend with 2.5 billion cars and light trucks, three times as many as today. Bike sharing has grown in popularity especially in the last several years to meet the demands for a viable sustainable form of environmentally-friendly transportation.

Cities are using bike sharing to integrate into their existing transportation systems so that it can be more readily become a viable daily transportation mode. Local residents and tourists can pick up a bicycle at one location within the city, use them, and then return them at another location for a small nominal fee. The pricing structure of bike sharing systems is often designed to encourage short-term use.

Bike sharing systems have proven effective in reducing urban congestion, encouraging healthy living, and allowing indviduals to benefit on their financial savings by reducing their transportation costs. It has grown so much in popularity that by the end of 2014, bike sharing systems have been made available in 855 cities around the world.

We will explore four bike sharing systems from different cities around the world and share their background, usage fees, and popular routes that can be enjoyed by riders.
